Black Friday Cosmetics

Black Friday is a time many of us eagerly anticipate. Who doesn’t love taking advantage of amazing offers and discounts? This special day—or more accurately, Black Week (or even Black Month!)—is the best time of the year to find the cosmetics, accessories, and other products we've been eyeing at bargain prices.

Black Week – What Does It Really Mean?

Black Week is essentially an extended version of Black Friday, the time of year when the biggest discounts are offered. In the UK and around the world, Black Friday has now expanded to a full week (in 2024, it will run from November 25 to December 1), giving us multiple days to enjoy these deals. Black Week allows us to shop for discounts without the overwhelming crowds and rush that can accompany Black Friday itself.

How Long Does Black Friday Last?

In theory, Black Friday is one day, but in practice, it often lasts much longer. Many stores, both physical and online, begin offering Black Friday discounts earlier in the week.

How Long is Black Week 2024?

Officially, Black Friday falls on Friday, November 29, but discounts generally start at the beginning of the week, November 25, 2024. Some stores even begin promotions as early as Monday, November 4. During this time, both online and physical stores offer special deals, making it unnecessary to wait until the last minute. It's a true shopping holiday for anyone planning big purchases or looking for great deals.

Why is it Helpful to Understand Black Week?

Because during these few days, you can truly save a lot!

How Long Do Black Friday Deals Last?

Though the name suggests Friday, it’s become common for special offers to run from the start of the week through the weekend. Additionally, some deals even extend into Monday, known as Cyber Monday, an online shopping day with further discounts. This means we have several days to take advantage of the best deals, though it’s worth remembering that popular products and the best deals can sell out quickly.

Why Has Black Friday Become Black Week?

Retailers know that by extending Black Friday, they can attract more customers who might otherwise miss the chance to shop on just one day. Competing for customers can be a challenge, so promotions, sales, and discounts start early. This extended shopping period offers deal hunters ample time to explore discounts without the urgency of a single-day sale, and for retailers, it means more opportunities to increase sales.

Black Friday – Where Did It All Begin?

We know Black Friday as the American tradition that brings huge promotions, but its origins are complex. Some say it started in the 1950s, when Americans took the day off after Thanksgiving to extend the holiday. Police officers in the 1960s referred to it as “Black Friday” because of the heavy traffic jams caused by holiday shopping.

However, by the 1980s, the term became positive for retailers. Traditionally, retailers marked losses in red in their ledgers and profits in black. After Thanksgiving, stores would finally be “in the black,” as Americans began their holiday shopping, leaving more money in stores.
